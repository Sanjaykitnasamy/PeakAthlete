Sport Events in UK

Past sporting events hosted in the UK

2022 Commonwealth Games

The 2022 Commonwealth Games, authoritatively known as the XXII Commonwealth Games and usually known as Birmingham 2022, was a worldwide multi-sport occurred in Birmingham, Britain between 28 July and 8 August 2022. The Games was the largest ever held, With 72 participating nations and over 1.3 million ticket sales. It was also the first event to combine the paralympic competition with more events for women than men and to have more events for women than men. Several trade shows and cultural festivals were held throughout the West Midlands in conjunction with the Games. There was also anesports competition. It was the last time the Games were held under Queen Elizabeth II before her death on September 8, exactly one month after the Games ended.

2019 Cricket World Cup

The 2019 ICC Cricket World Cup was the 12th Cricket World Cup, a quadrennial One Day International (ODI) cricket tournament contested by men's national teams and organized by the International Cricket Council (ICC). It was hosted between 30 May and 14 July across 10 venues in England and a single venue in Wales with the tournament being the fifth time that England had hosted the World Cup while for Wales it was their third. England and New Zealand won their respective semi-finals to qualify for the final, which was played at Lord's in London. The final ended in a tie after the match ended with both teams scoring 241 runs, followed by the first Super Over in an ODI; England won the title, their first, on the boundary countback rule after the Super Over also finished level.

2012 London Olympics

London 2012 Olympic Games, athletic festival held in London that took place July 27–August 12, 2012. The London Games were the 27th occurrence of the modern Olympic Games. In 2012 London became the first city to host the modern Games three times, having previously been the site of the 1908 and 1948 Olympic Games.

2015 Rugby World Cup

The quadrennial rugby union world championship, the Rugby World Cup, was held in 2015. It was the eighth Rugby World Cup. The competition was facilitated by Britain from 18 September to 31 October. The World Cup had no new teams for the first time ever.New Zealand were the first team to retain their title and the first to win for a third time.

2017 World Athletic Championships

The sixteenth IAAF World Championships were held at London Stadium in London, United Kingdom, from August 4 to 13 for the 2017 event. London was authoritatively granted the titles on 11 November 2011.

1966 FIFA World Cup

The eighth FIFA World Cup, a quadrennial football competition for senior men's national teams, took place in 1966. From July 11 to July 30, 1966, it was played in England. England won its first and only World Cup title by defeating West Germany 4–2 in the final. The first FIFA World Cup to be held in an English-speaking region was in 1966. The final was held at Wembley Stadium, which had a capacity of 98,600 and was one of eight stadiums across England. With 70 nations participating, the 1966 event had the most teams of any international tournament to date. For the first time, certain matches of the World Cup were broadcast via satellite to nations on other continents. The final was the last to be shown entirely in black and white, and the BBC broadcast it locally.

Upcoming sporting events hosted in the UK

2023 Wheelchair Rugby European Championship

Cardiff, Wales will host the 2023 Wheelchair Rugby European Championship from 3rd to 7th May, which promises to be an action-packed and thrilling weekend of rugby unlike any other. Eight European teams will compete in the championship, including Paralympic gold medalist Great Britain competing on home turf. Not only is the European trophy up for grabs, but direct entry into the Paralympic Games in Paris in 2024 is also up for grabs for the winners.

2023 World Para Swimming Championships

Manchester will host the 11th Para Swimming World Championships from July 31 to August 6, 2023. The Manchester Aquatics Centre is where the competition will take place. After Glasgow 2015 and London 2019, the World Championships will be held in Great Britain for the third time in Manchester in 2023.

2023 ICT Canoe Slalom World Championships

The World Championship competition will once more take place at the Lee Valley White Water Centre in England from September 19th –24th , 2023. This will be the venue's first major international competition since the 2019 ICF World Cup, in which Great Britain won an amazing seven titles, including four golds.

2023 FIG Trampoline world Championships

The 37th FIG Trampoline Gymnastics World Championships will be held in Birmingham, England from November 9th –19th, 2023, at the Utilita Arena, and will feature athletes from all over the world.

2030 T20 Cricket World Cup

The 2030 ICC Men's T20 World Cup will be co-hosted by England, Scotland, and Ireland. The International Cricket Council (ICC) made the announcement as it announced the hosts of each men's white-ball tournament from 2024 to 2031.The 2030 event's schedule and host venues still need to be finalized.

2024 UEFA Champions League Final

The final match of the 2023–24 UEFA Champions League will be the 2024 UEFA Champions League final. This will be the 69th season of UEFA's premier club football tournament and the 32nd season since it was renamed the UEFA Champions League from the European Champion Clubs' Cup. It will take place on June 1, 2024, at Wembley Stadium in London, England. As a result of the 2020 final's postponement and relocation, London will host the 2024 final instead. The winners will be eligible to compete in the 2024 UEFA Super Cup against the winners of the 2023–24 UEFA Europa League.
